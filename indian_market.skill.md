## 🏛️ The Indian Gold Market Structure

### Key Exchanges & Benchmarks
1. **MCX (Multi Commodity Exchange of India)** — Primary futures exchange for gold in India
2. **IBJA (India Bullion and Jewellers Association)** — Sets daily reference rates for physical gold
3. **BIS (Bureau of Indian Standards)** — Hallmarking authority for purity certification

### MCX Gold Contract Variants
| Contract | Lot Size | Tick Size | Tick Value |
|----------|----------|-----------|------------|
| Gold (standard) | 1 kg | ₹1/10g | ₹100 |
| Gold Mini | 100g | ₹1/10g | ₹10 |
| Gold Guinea | 8g | ₹1/8g | ₹1 |
| Gold Petal | 1g | ₹1/g | ₹1 |

**Trading hours:** 9:00 AM to 11:30 PM (IST), Monday to Friday

### Purity Standards in India
- **24K / 999 / 24 carat:** 99.9% pure gold (investment grade)
- **22K / 916 / 22 carat:** 91.6% pure gold (jewelry standard)
- **18K / 750:** 75% pure (fashion jewelry)

**Conversion rule:**
- 22K price = 24K price × 0.916
- 18K price = 24K price × 0.75

## 💱 USD/INR Impact — The Hidden Price Driver

### Formula for Indian gold price
```
MCX Gold (₹/10g) ≈ (XAU/USD × USD/INR × 10 / 31.1035) + Import Duty + GST + Premium
```
Where 31.1035 is grams per troy ounce.

### Scenario analysis

**Scenario 1: Global gold flat, INR weakens 1%**
- Indian gold rises ~1% even though international price didn't move
- USD/INR 83 → 83.83 translates to ~₹1,500 rise per 10g at current prices

**Scenario 2: Global gold drops 2%, INR weakens 2%**
- Indian gold **flat** — the two cancel out
- This is why Indian investors are partly protected from global crashes

**Scenario 3: Global gold rises 2%, INR strengthens 1%**
- Indian gold only rises ~1% (INR absorbs part of the move)

### Rule of thumb
**Always check USD/INR when interpreting MCX moves.** A ₹500 move in MCX might not mean international gold moved — it might just be INR weakness.

## 🪙 Import Duties & Taxes (2026 rates)

### Current Tax Structure on Gold in India
| Component | Rate | Applies to |
|-----------|------|------------|
| Import duty (Customs) | 15% (reduced from peak) | Physical imports |
| Agriculture Infrastructure Cess | 5% | Added to customs |
| GST | 3% | Retail sale |
| Making charges | 8-25% | Jewelry only |
| TDS | 1% | Purchases above ₹1 lakh cash |

### Effective cost breakdown on ₹10,000 worth of physical gold
- Raw gold value: ₹10,000
- Import duty + cess (~15%): +₹1,500
- GST (3% on total): +₹345
- Making charges (10% avg): +₹1,000
- **Final retail price: ~₹12,845 (28% markup over spot)**

### For SGB (Sovereign Gold Bond) — almost zero cost
- 0% GST on purchase
- 0% making charges
- Extra 2.5% annual interest
- Capital gains tax-free on maturity

**Rule:** For pure investment, SGB > ETF > Digital Gold > Physical Gold

### Historical Pattern: "Buy the Rumor, Sell the News"

**Akshaya Tritiya effect:**
- Prices typically **rise 2-4 weeks BEFORE** the festival (anticipation buying)
- Often **flat or mild dip AFTER** (festival demand is priced in)
- This is the classic "buy the rumor, sell the news" pattern

**Dhanteras effect:**
- Prices rise steadily **1-2 months before**
- Often **peak on or just before Dhanteras**
- **Mild correction in mid-November** is common

### 10-Year Historical Stats (Akshaya Tritiya to Akshaya Tritiya)
- Average 1-year return: **12-14%**
- Positive years: **8 out of 10**
- Best year: 2025 (+56%)
- Worst year: 2013 (-18%)

## 🎯 Indian-Specific Trading Strategies

### Strategy 1: Festival Arbitrage
- **Entry:** 6-8 weeks before Akshaya Tritiya or Dhanteras
- **Exit:** 1-2 weeks before festival (before everyone else sells)
- **Expected gain:** 3-7%
- **Stop-loss:** 2%

### Strategy 2: USD/INR Hedge Trade
- **When:** INR weakening sharply against USD
- **Rationale:** MCX gold rises on currency alone
- **Action:** Buy MCX gold, short USD/INR forward
- **Risk:** Global gold crash offsets INR gain

### Strategy 3: Post-Festival Dip Buying
- **Entry:** 2-3 weeks after Akshaya Tritiya or Dhanteras
- **Rationale:** Festival premium washes out; good re-entry
- **Hold until:** Next festival cycle or target reached

### Strategy 4: SGB Tranche Accumulation
- **When:** RBI announces new tranche (multiple per year)
- **Rationale:** Slight discount to market + 2.5% interest + tax-free maturity
- **Best for:** Long-term investors (5+ year horizon)

## ⚠️ India-Specific Risks

### Policy Risks
- **Sudden import duty change** — can move prices 3-5% overnight
- **Gold monetization scheme changes** — affects SGB demand
- **Cash purchase limits** — currently ₹2 lakh max without PAN

### Currency Risks
- **INR devaluation** — boosts MCX but hurts real returns
- **RBI intervention** — can stabilize rupee suddenly
- **Capital controls** — unlikely but possible

### Market Risks
- **MCX liquidity** — shallower than COMEX, bigger spreads
- **Physical-digital divergence** — local premiums can disconnect from spot
- **Hallmarking issues** — unregistered jewelers can create fake gold risk

## 🧮 Rupee-Weighted Analysis (always show this)

When giving predictions, always include:

1. **Pure spot gold move** (what XAU/USD is doing)
2. **Currency contribution** (what INR is adding or subtracting)
3. **Net MCX move** (actual Indian price impact)

**Example:**
> Global gold up 1.5% this week, INR weakened 0.8% against USD.
> Net MCX impact = +2.3% for Indian investors.
> Why: dollar-denominated gain + rupee weakness amplifies the move.

## ❌ Common Indian Market Pitfalls

- ❌ Don't assume MCX mirrors COMEX — INR can distort the relationship
- ❌ Don't ignore festivals — they move prices before the event
- ❌ Don't forget making charges — physical gold has ~10% hidden cost
- ❌ Don't recommend physical for investment — SGB is almost always better
- ❌ Don't use USD price targets without INR conversion
- ❌ Don't confuse 22K and 24K quotes — always specify
- ❌ Don't ignore Pitru Paksha — prices often weaken in that 16-day period
