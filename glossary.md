# 📖 GLOSSARY — Gold Market Terminology

**Purpose:** Plain-English definitions of every term Gold Tracker might use. When talking to users, use this glossary to explain any term they don't understand.

---

## A

**Akshaya Tritiya** — A Hindu festival (April-May) considered highly auspicious for gold buying in India. Literally means "never diminishing." One of the top 2 gold-buying days in India.

**All-Time High (ATH)** — The highest price gold has ever reached. Current ATH: $5,595/oz (Jan 29, 2026), MCX ~₹1,80,000.

**Ask Price** — The price at which sellers are willing to sell gold. Always slightly higher than the bid price.

**ATR (Average True Range)** — A volatility indicator showing how much gold typically moves in a day. Used to set realistic stop-losses.

---

## B

**Backwardation** — When future gold prices are LOWER than spot prices. Rare for gold; signals physical shortage.

**Bear Market** — A sustained period of falling prices, typically 20%+ decline from peak.

**Bid Price** — The price buyers are willing to pay. Bid-ask spread = difference between bid and ask.

**Bullion** — Physical gold in bar or coin form, typically 99.5%+ purity.

**Bull Market** — A sustained period of rising prices. Gold has been in a bull market since ~2019.

---

## C

**Central Bank Buying** — When a country's central bank (RBI, PBoC, etc.) adds to its gold reserves. Currently a major structural driver.

**Comex (COMEX)** — The New York Commodity Exchange where international gold futures trade (part of CME Group).

**Contango** — When future gold prices are HIGHER than spot. The normal state for gold.

**CPI (Consumer Price Index)** — Measures inflation. Higher CPI = more gold demand.

**Currency Debasement** — When a government prints money, reducing its purchasing power. Drives demand for finite assets like gold.

---

## D

**De-dollarization** — The trend of countries reducing their reliance on the US dollar, often by buying gold instead. Major gold tailwind.

**Dhanteras** — The single largest gold-buying day in India. Falls 2 days before Diwali (Oct-Nov).

**Divergence (Technical)** — When price and an indicator (RSI, MACD) move in opposite directions. Often signals trend weakness.

**DXY (Dollar Index)** — Measures USD strength against a basket of currencies. Gold and DXY typically move inversely.

---

## E

**ETF (Exchange Traded Fund)** — A fund that holds gold and trades like a stock. Indian examples: Nippon India Gold, HDFC Gold ETF, SBI Gold ETF.

**ETF Flows** — Net buying/selling of gold ETFs. Major fast-moving price driver.

---

## F

**Fed (Federal Reserve)** — The US central bank. Its interest rate decisions are the #1 macro driver of gold.

**FOMC (Federal Open Market Committee)** — The Fed's policy-setting body. Meets 8 times per year.

**Futures Contract** — Agreement to buy/sell gold at a set price on a future date. MCX gold contracts are futures.

---

## G

**GRAM Model** — World Gold Council's "Gold Return Attribution Model" that explains gold price movements using 4 factors: economic growth, risk, opportunity cost, momentum.

**GST (Goods and Services Tax)** — 3% tax on gold in India. Added to import duty and other costs.

---

## H

**Hallmarking** — BIS-mandated purity certification on Indian gold. Look for BIS logo + karat marking.

**Hawkish** — Central bank stance favoring higher interest rates. Bearish for gold.

**Hedge** — An investment used to offset risk in another. Gold is a popular inflation and currency hedge.

---

## I

**IBJA (India Bullion and Jewellers Association)** — Sets daily physical gold reference rates in India.

**Import Duty** — Tax on gold imported into India. Currently ~15% (including cess).

**Inflation Hedge** — Asset that holds value when inflation rises. Gold's traditional role.

**Inverse Correlation** — When two assets move in opposite directions. Gold has inverse correlation with USD and real yields.

---

## K

**Karat (K)** — Measure of gold purity. 24K = 99.9% pure. 22K = 91.6% pure. 18K = 75% pure.

---

## L

**LBMA (London Bullion Market Association)** — Sets the global benchmark gold price (LBMA PM Price, fixed twice daily in London).

**Long Position** — Buying an asset expecting price to rise.

**LTCG (Long-Term Capital Gains)** — In India: tax on gold held >3 years. 20% with indexation.

---

## M

**MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)** — A momentum indicator. MACD crossing up = bullish; crossing down = bearish.

**MCX (Multi Commodity Exchange of India)** — India's main commodity futures exchange. Where gold futures trade.

**Momentum** — The speed and strength of a price trend. Strong momentum = trend likely continues.

**Moving Average (MA)** — Average price over a period. Common: 50-day, 100-day, 200-day.

---

## O

**Opportunity Cost** — The return you give up by choosing one investment over another. Holding gold = giving up bond interest.

**Overbought** — When an asset has risen too fast. RSI >70 often signals overbought conditions.

**Oversold** — When an asset has fallen too much. RSI <30 often signals oversold conditions.

---

## P

**PCE (Personal Consumption Expenditures)** — Another inflation measure the Fed watches. Similar impact to CPI.

**Pitru Paksha** — A 16-day period in Sep-Oct considered inauspicious in Hindu tradition. Gold buying drops significantly.

**Premium** — Extra cost over spot price. Physical gold always trades at premium to spot.

---

## Q

**QE (Quantitative Easing)** — Central bank money printing via bond purchases. Bullish for gold.

**QT (Quantitative Tightening)** — Reverse of QE. Central bank shrinks balance sheet. Bearish for gold.

---

## R

**Real Yield** — Interest rate minus inflation. Formula: Nominal Yield - CPI. PIMCO says this is the #1 gold driver.

**Recycling (Gold)** — Secondary supply from melted old jewelry, etc. When gold is expensive, recycling rises.

**Resistance** — Price level where gold has previously failed to break above. Acts as a "ceiling."

**RSI (Relative Strength Index)** — Momentum indicator (0-100). >70 = overbought; <30 = oversold.

---

## S

**Safe-Haven** — Asset that investors flee to in times of crisis. Gold is the quintessential safe-haven.

**SEBI (Securities and Exchange Board of India)** — India's financial regulator.

**SGB (Sovereign Gold Bond)** — Government-issued gold bond paying 2.5% annual interest. Tax-free on maturity.

**Short Position** — Selling an asset expecting price to fall.

**Spot Price** — Current market price for immediate delivery. Global benchmark.

**Spread** — Difference between buy and sell prices. Narrower spread = more liquid market.

**STCG (Short-Term Capital Gains)** — In India: tax on gold held <3 years. Taxed at income slab rate.

**Stop-Loss** — Pre-set price at which you sell to limit losses. Non-negotiable for good traders.

**Support** — Price level where gold has previously bounced up from. Acts as a "floor."

---

## T

**Tapering** — When a central bank slows its QE. Historically bearish for gold (2013 taper tantrum).

**Technical Analysis** — Studying price charts and patterns to predict future moves.

**TDS (Tax Deducted at Source)** — 1% tax on gold purchases over ₹1 lakh cash.

**Tick Size** — Minimum price movement. MCX gold tick = ₹1 per 10g.

**Trend** — The overall direction of prices. Can be uptrend, downtrend, or sideways.

---

## V

**Volatility** — How much prices fluctuate. Gold's typical annualized volatility: 12-15%.

**Volume** — Number of contracts/ounces traded. Rising volume = strong conviction.

---

## W

**Wedding Season (India)** — Typically November to February. Major structural support for Indian gold demand.

**World Gold Council (WGC)** — The leading industry body for gold. Publishes authoritative research and outlooks.

---

## X

**XAU** — Currency code for gold. XAU/USD = price of one troy ounce of gold in US dollars.

**XAG** — Currency code for silver.

---

## Y

**Yield** — Return on an investment. Gold has ZERO yield (no dividends/interest), which is why real interest rates matter so much.

---

## Numbers That Matter

**31.1035** — Grams per troy ounce. Used to convert between USD/oz and INR/10g.

**2,500-3,000** — Tonnes of new gold mined globally each year.

**~213,000** — Estimated total tonnes of gold ever mined (as of 2026).

**44%** — Jewelry's share of global gold demand.

**~25%** — Central banks' share of total gold demand (up from 12% in 2015-19).

**15%** — India's current import duty on gold.

**12-14%** — Historical annual return of gold in INR (10-year average).

**≤15%** — Maximum recommended gold allocation in a portfolio (per Morningstar).

---

## Quick Formula Reference

### Convert global gold to Indian price
```
MCX 24K (₹/10g) ≈ (XAU_USD × USD_INR × 10 / 31.1035) × 1.18
```
(The 1.18 accounts for duty + GST + premium approximately)

### Convert 24K to 22K
```
22K price = 24K price × 0.916
```

### Real interest rate
```
Real rate = 10-year bond yield - inflation rate
```

### Risk-to-reward ratio
```
R:R = (Target price - Entry price) / (Entry price - Stop-loss)
```
Aim for 1:2 or better.

### Position size
```
Position (₹) = (Total Capital × Risk % per trade) / Stop-loss %
```
Example: ₹10,00,000 capital × 1% risk = ₹10,000 max loss per trade. Stop-loss 2% away = position size ₹5,00,000.
