# SKILL: Technical Analysis — Chart Patterns & Momentum

**Trigger:** User asks about "chart pattern," "resistance," "support," "trend," "moving average," "RSI," "breakout," or any technical chart question.

**Purpose:** Read price charts, identify technical signals, and forecast short-term movement based on price action alone.

---

## Core Technical Indicators to Check

### 1. Moving Averages (MA)
- **50-day MA** — Short-term trend
- **100-day MA** — Medium-term trend
- **200-day MA** — Long-term trend

**Signal rules:**
- Price above 200-MA = long-term uptrend (bullish)
- Price below 200-MA = long-term downtrend (bearish)
- **Golden Cross** (50-MA crosses above 200-MA) = strong bullish signal
- **Death Cross** (50-MA crosses below 200-MA) = strong bearish signal

### 2. Support & Resistance Levels
- **Support** = price level where gold has previously bounced up from
- **Resistance** = price level where gold has previously failed to break above
- **Rule:** When price breaks resistance with volume, resistance becomes new support
- **Rule:** When price breaks support, support becomes new resistance

**How to find them:**
- Look at 6-month and 1-year price history
- Mark prices where gold has touched and reversed 2+ times
- Recent all-time high ($5,595 in Jan 2026) is a key resistance
- Psychological levels: $5,000, $4,500, $4,000 for XAU/USD; ₹80,000, ₹75,000, ₹70,000 for MCX

### 3. RSI (Relative Strength Index)
- **RSI > 70** = Overbought → likely pullback coming (bearish short-term)
- **RSI < 30** = Oversold → likely bounce coming (bullish short-term)
- **RSI 40-60** = Neutral range

### 4. MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)
- **MACD line crossing above signal line** = bullish signal
- **MACD line crossing below signal line** = bearish signal
- **Divergence** (price makes new high but MACD doesn't) = weakening trend

### 5. Volume
- **Rising price + rising volume** = strong bullish trend
- **Rising price + falling volume** = weakening rally (watch for reversal)
- **Falling price + rising volume** = strong bearish trend

## Key Chart Patterns to Recognize

### Bullish Patterns
1. **Cup and Handle** — strong continuation pattern
2. **Ascending Triangle** — breakout usually upward
3. **Bull Flag** — brief consolidation in uptrend
4. **Double Bottom** — reversal from downtrend

### Bearish Patterns
1. **Head and Shoulders** — reversal from uptrend
2. **Descending Triangle** — breakout usually downward
3. **Double Top** — reversal from uptrend
4. **Bearish Flag** — brief pause in downtrend

### Neutral Patterns
1. **Symmetrical Triangle** — breakout can go either way
2. **Rectangle / Consolidation** — wait for breakout

## Workflow

### Step 1: Fetch Current Technical Data
Use web_search:
- "XAU USD technical analysis today"
- "MCX gold support resistance levels"
- "gold RSI MACD current"
- "gold chart pattern analysis [current week]"

### Step 2: Identify Trend
- Check if price is above or below 50, 100, 200 MA
- Look at last 3 months of price action
- Classify: **Strong Uptrend / Uptrend / Sideways / Downtrend / Strong Downtrend**

### Step 3: Mark Key Levels
For Indian market (MCX 24K per 10g):
- Nearest resistance above current price
- Nearest support below current price
- Major psychological level nearby

### Step 4: Check Momentum Indicators
- RSI reading
- MACD state
- Volume trend (rising/falling)

### Step 5: Look for Patterns
- Any recognizable pattern forming?
- Time to pattern completion?
- Expected direction of breakout?

### Step 6: Generate Technical View
Combine all signals into a single technical score:
- +2 = Strong bullish technicals
- +1 = Mildly bullish
- 0 = Neutral / wait for breakout
- −1 = Mildly bearish
- −2 = Strong bearish

## Indian Market Specifics

### Key MCX Technical Levels to Remember
- All-time highs often psychological
- Watch ₹5,000 intervals (₹75K, ₹80K, ₹85K)
- RSI on daily timeframe most reliable for swing trading

### MCX vs International Divergence
- Sometimes MCX moves differently than XAU/USD due to:
  - USD/INR movement
  - Import duty changes
  - Domestic demand spikes (festival season)
- **If MCX is rising while XAU/USD is flat:** likely rupee weakness driving it
- **If MCX is flat while XAU/USD is rising:** rupee strength is offsetting the gain

## Common Pitfalls

- ❌ Don't rely on technicals alone — always combine with fundamentals
- ❌ Don't ignore volume — price moves without volume often reverse
- ❌ Don't chase breakouts blindly — wait for retest of broken level
- ❌ Don't set stop-losses too tight — gold is volatile; use 1.5-3% below support
- ❌ Don't ignore USD/INR — it's a major technical factor for Indian gold
